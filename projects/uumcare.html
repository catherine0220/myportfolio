UUM Care Mobile Application

Final Year Project (2023-2024).
UI/UX mental health support mobile application for UUM Students.

Project Overview

The UUM Care mobile application was developed to address the rising mental health challenges among students at Universiti Utara Malaysia (UUM). The app integrates Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT) principles to help students manage depression through features like mental health assessments, guided meditation, task management, and counseling bookings.

Connect to App

Formative Evaluation

A formative evaluation was conducted with four experts to assess the app's content and interface. Key feedback included the need for an emergency button, real-time chat with counselors, and improved DASS test instructions. Based on expert recommendations, Phase 1 amendments were successfully completed.

Summative Evaluation

The summative evaluation involved user testing with 10 UUM students to assess the app's usability, effectiveness, and satisfaction. Results showed high user satisfaction, with 70% of respondents strongly agreeing that the app was useful in managing depression.

Future Improvements

With both Phase 1 and Phase 2 completed, future improvements will focus on refining the app based on ongoing user feedback and incorporating more interactive elements to enhance long-term support.
